    Experience

    Scientific Internship

    National Laboratory of Scientific Computing - LNCC, Petrópolis, RJ
    (May 2014 - March 2016)

    Did scientific internship at the LNCC in the area of modeling of systems and control. The work in the LNCC had as objective make a simulation of the PageRank algorithm, responsible for ranking the pages in the Google search engine.

    The work began with a bibliographical review of the latest articles related to ranking algorithms and included the article "The anatomy of a large-scale hypertextual web search engine" published in 1998 by Sergey Brin and Lawrence Page. During the study of the bibliography, was necessary made a study of some extra curricular subjects of the Information Technology course, for example: stochastic systems, Markov chains and linear systems. Simulations of the algorithm were done using Matlab, where the results were compared with different models and methods proposed in the literature.

    At the end of the work, the analyzes of the results were made and other models and methods that would also be valid for PageRank simulation were proposed. With the results obtained, the methods and models were proved to be efficient in case of an implementation at a real environment. Also, due to the distributed model, they could be implemented in a cluster making the ranking even less costly.

    Software Development Internship

    Laboratory of Intelligent Systems and Robotics - SIRLab, Petrópolis, RJ
    (April 2014 - December 2015)

    Worked at SIRLab as a software developer in C ++ language at the VSS-SDK project. The project consisted of developing an autonomous robot soccer system for the IEEE Very Small Size Soccer category. Among the areas that constituted the development of the SIRSoccer system, was in charge of the systems and control researsh and development.

    The work began with the study of topics of analytical geometry for the creation of a namespace with functions that would calculate distances and angulations. After good operation of the base strategy, capable of making estimates close to the real model, we began the research of the numerical model of the robot system and controllers ables to overcome abrupt changes in their operating points. The problems occasioned by the system failures were treated with a classical, linear and univariable control, the PID.

    With time, was realized that the use of the PID only could be effective if it was embedded in the robot to make adjustments in the angular velocity of the wheels. And in addition to the PID, a nonlinear control would be required for the Cartesian coordinates and angulation.
